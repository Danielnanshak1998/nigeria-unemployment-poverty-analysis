# 🇳🇬 Nigeria Unemployment & Poverty Crisis — Data Analysis 2023

## Project Overview
A state-by-state Excel analysis of Nigeria's unemployment and poverty 
crisis using data from the NBS Labour Force Survey 2023 and NBS Poverty 
& Inequality Report 2019. This project answers four key questions 
relevant to policymakers, NGOs, and development organizations working 
in Nigeria.

## Q1 — Which states have the highest unemployment rates, and is there a North/South pattern?

**Finding:** Southern states dominate the top of the unemployment rankings — 
contrary to what most people expect.

- 🔴 **Abia (South East)** leads the country at **18.7%** — classified High Risk
- 🔴 **FCT (North Central)** follows at **14.1%** — driven by public sector saturation
- 🔴 **Rivers (South South)** ranks 3rd at **13.4%** — despite being an oil-producing state
- The **top 14 states** by unemployment are all Southern or FCT
- **North average: 8.41%** vs **South average: 10.09%**
- Lagos, despite being Nigeria's commercial capital, has the **lowest unemployment at 5.5%**

> **Insight:** High unemployment in the South is partly explained by a larger formal 
> job-seeking population — more people are looking for paid employment rather than 
> defaulting to subsistence agriculture as in the North.

---

## Q2 — How does poverty rate correlate with unemployment by state?

**Finding:** High unemployment and high poverty do NOT always go together — 
the North proves that you can be employed and still be extremely poor.

- **North West** has the **lowest unemployment (7.7% avg)** but the **highest poverty (71.4% avg)**
- **Sokoto** has only 7.5% unemployment but **87.7% poverty** — the highest in the country
- **Jigawa** has 7.9% unemployment but **87% poverty**
- **Lagos** has 5.5% unemployment and only **4.5% poverty** — the best performing state
- **Rivers** has 13.4% unemployment but only **10.8% poverty** — oil wealth cushions living standards
- **South East** average: 11.7% unemployment but only 37.4% poverty

> **Insight:** In Nigeria, poverty is more strongly linked to the **type of work** 
> (subsistence agriculture vs formal employment) than to unemployment rates alone.

---

## Q3 — Which states have the worst youth unemployment?

**Finding:** Only 3 states exceed 20% youth unemployment — and all three are in the South.

- 🥇 **Abia**: **28.4%** — nearly double the national average of 15.51%
- 🥈 **FCT**: **22.8%** — driven by graduate oversupply relative to government jobs
- 🥉 **Rivers**: **21.6%** — youth locked out despite oil sector presence
- **Abia's youth unemployment (28.4%) is nearly 3x Lagos (9.8%)**
- **3 states** have youth unemployment above 20%
- **National youth unemployment average: 15.51%**
- States below the national average include Kogi (15.2%) and Taraba (15.3%)

> **Insight:** The youth unemployment crisis is concentrated in South Eastern states 
> where tertiary education rates are high but formal job creation has not kept pace.

---

## Q4 — What is the relationship between informal employment and poverty?

**Finding:** The higher the informal employment rate in a region, 
the higher the poverty rate — they move together almost perfectly.

- **North West** has the highest informal employment at **96.3%** and highest poverty at **71.4%**
- **North East** follows at **96.1%** informal employment and **68.9%** poverty
- **South West** has the lowest informal employment at **85.5%** and lowest poverty at **22.0%**
- **Sokoto** tops the informal employment list at **97.8%** — with 87.7% poverty
- **Lagos** has the lowest informal employment at **78.2%** — and only 4.5% poverty
- National average underemployment: **18.4%** — meaning millions who work are not fully utilized

> **Insight:** Informal employment is not just a labour market characteristic — 
> it is a poverty trap. States must transition workers from subsistence agriculture 
> into formal, productive employment to break the poverty cycle.
